A Cause in Satisfaction

Explanation

My cause is to understand how satisfied people are in general, how people feel about their lives currently and why. Most people strive to be happy in life, but many are not. Especially in a world where the social context is changing so drastically with new technology and social structures, how do people feel about their lives at the current moment? I think this is important with the social media wave that has taken over the world. Many people try to construct a "perfect" profile where they seem happy and content, but they may not be at all in real life.

We have to ask ourselves, how do we measure satisfaction or happiness? Is there a numerical measure for it? Because happiness is so subjective, the answer can vary depending on the person you ask. For someone who is economically impoverished, the more money they have may correlate directly to how happy they say they are. If you look at another person who "should" be happy, say they have a supporting family, are economically well off and healthy, but says they are depressed and unsatisfied with their lives. Why is that? Is there an overarching reason?

I think that if there is a reason, it is imperative we understand why these feelings of satisfaction exist or do not exist to better understand ourselves and each other. This is important to me because it serves to answer why we get up in the morning and what we are striving for. Basically I, like many others, want to be happy and want to know what that entails and what might distract me from it. There is a lot to understand about the behavior of people based on their pursuit of happiness: what they need to be happy, what they work towards to gain happiness, and more.
