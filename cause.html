A Cause in Satisfaction

My cause is to understand how satisfied people are in general, how people feel about their lives currently and why. Most people strive to be happy in life, but many are not. Especially in a world where the social context is changing so drastically with new technology and social structures, how do people feel about their lives at the current moment?

We have to ask ourselves, how do we measure satisfaction or happiness? Is there a numerical measure for it? Because happiness is so subjective, the answer can vary depending on the person you ask. For someone who is economically impoverished, the more money they have may correlate directly to how happy they say they are. If you look at another person who "should" be happy, say they have a supporting family, are economically well off and healthy, but says they are depressed and unsatisfied with their lives. Why is that? Is there an overarching reason?

I think that if there is a reason, it is imperative we understand why these feelings of satisfaction exist or do not exist to better understand ourselves and each other. This is important to me because it serves to answer why we get up in the morning and what we are striving for. Basically I, like many others, want to be happy and want to know what that entails and what might distract me from it.

The first piece of data I want to work with focuses on suicide rates (Numerical) by country. Because the act of intentional self harm is so extreme, a connection to individual unsatisfaction can be made more easily than a more general descriptor of a population. To understand why some are happy, it can be important to understand why some people are not happy.

The data is marked by time (Numerical) that give us a historical context of what is happening in that part of the world in that year, and also distinguished by Male, Female, or Total (String).
