Misunderstanding About Happiness

One misunderstanding about happiness is that money either does or does not bring you happiness.

Debunking the Myth

To debunk this myth, I think that it is important to first define money, and how it is used. Money in our society is simply pieces of paper that we can use to purchase goods or services. Those that feel strongly against the idea that money brings happiness have truth in their feelings, and the same for those who feel stronly that money does bring happiness. The alternate explanation that can be given is that money does bring happiness up to a certain point. One study says that the ideal income level is around $70,000-90,000 for peak happiness. Note that this is not close to the million dollar level or the poverty level. Everyone has different amounts of goods and services that provide what they need to be happy, but on average this income level can sustain that amount. Anything less will may make the person want more money to be happy, while more money will buy goods and services frivolous to their needs. Increased income may be the result of less free time, higher stress and workload. Overall there is not a definite answer that money definitely brings happiness or definitely does not. It can very well bring happiness or not depending on the individual.
